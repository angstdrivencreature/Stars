Venus

Second planet from the Sun and our closest planetary neighbor, Venus is similar in structure and size to Earth, but it is now a very different world.

Venus spins slowly in the opposite direction most planets do, with one day lasting 243 Earth days.

Its thick atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system—with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead. Glimpses below the clouds reveal volcanoes and deformed mountains. The planet’s surface temperature is about 465 degrees Celsius—hot enough to melt lead.

Many scientists believe water once existed on the surface. Future Venus explorers will search for evidence of an ancient ocean.
